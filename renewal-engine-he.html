Learning Loop - V2 (Deliberately Deferred)

L4 is the only layer entirely deferred to V2 - and that's intentional. You can't optimize a model with zero training data. V1's job is to generate the interaction signals that L4 needs: who dismissed, who engaged but didn't convert, who converted on the first push vs. the dashboard widget. After one renewal cycle (~6 months), we'll have enough data to build meaningful segments.

  • Dismiss patterns: Did they dismiss the widget, the push, or both? On the first cycle or second? This tells us whether the surface is wrong (channel problem) or the timing is wrong (window problem).
  • Engage-no-convert: Customers who tap "See My Quote" but don't bind a policy. Is the drop-off at the quote stage (price problem) or the application stage (friction problem)? This shapes V2 interventions.
  • Timing optimization: V1 uses a fixed 30-day window. V2 tests whether 30-day, 45-day, or 60-day windows convert differently across customer segments. Early data suggests younger customers decide faster (shorter window) while multi-policy holders deliberate longer.

Edge Case: Dismissed

Customer ignores widget and push this cycle. State resets to date_captured and the engine waits for the next renewal window. After two consecutive dismissals, the customer is suppressed until they show fresh intent (browsing the car tab, updating their profile). No nagging, ever.

Edge Case: Browsed But Didn't Convert

Customer taps "See My Quote" but drops off mid-flow. In V1, they stay in activated state - the dashboard widget persists with their saved quote progress. In V2, L4 (Learn) analyzes the drop-off point to determine if it's a pricing issue or a friction issue, and adjusts the next offer.

"As Promised" - Delivering on the Commitment

The notification references the earlier conversation: "As promised - I checked rates." This transforms a push notification from an interruption into a follow-through. The customer opted into this months ago; Lemonade is keeping its word.

UX: Promised notification ≠ unsolicited marketing

Three Personalization Hooks

Customer's name ("Sarah"), a concrete dollar figure ("$32/mo"), and the bundle pitch ("renters becomes practically free"). Each element is pulled from existing Lemonade data - no new input required from the customer. Personalized pushes drive 3× higher open rates.

Metric: 3× open rate with personalization

Single Shot - No Nagging

V1 sends exactly one push notification and one parallel email per renewal cycle. If the customer doesn't engage with either, the dashboard widget remains visible as a passive fallback. This discipline builds trust: Lemonade reaches out once across two channels, then waits. No drip campaigns, no guilt.

Insight: Restraint is a feature, not a limitation

Risk Mitigation
Anticipated Risks & Mitigations
High Risk
Low Capture Rate

Customers may not share or know their renewal date. Mitigation: Month-level granularity (not exact date) reduces friction. Uncertain customers are inferred from purchase timing (see Flow 1). The Dashboard Calendar Widget provides a persistent secondary path.

Medium Risk
Push Notification Fatigue

Customers may perceive the notification as spam. Mitigation: V1 sends exactly one notification per cycle. "As promised" framing connects to the opt-in moment. Dismiss-twice suppression prevents re-targeting unhappy users.

Medium Risk
Price Competitiveness

Lemonade's car rates may not beat the customer's current carrier. Mitigation: Lead with estimated savings, not guaranteed. If Lemonade can't beat the rate, Maya acknowledges it honestly - building trust for the next cycle. The bundle discount often offsets small price differences.

Medium Risk
State Availability Gap

Car insurance only available in ~10 states (~60% of base). Mitigation: Only collect and activate for eligible customers. For ineligible states, store the data - it becomes instantly valuable when Lemonade expands to that state.

Medium Risk
Regulatory Compliance

Insurance marketing is regulated by state - claims like "save ~$32/mo" in push notifications may face scrutiny in certain jurisdictions. Mitigation: Legal review required on all push copy and savings estimates before launch. Savings framed as "estimated" (not guaranteed). State-specific copy variants where required.
