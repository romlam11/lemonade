Core Insight

Car insurance has hard renewal cycles - 6 or 12 months. A perfectly crafted offer sent 3 months after renewal gets ignored. The same offer sent 30 days before renewal, when the customer is evaluating options and their current carrier is sending renewal notices, converts at dramatically higher rates. Yet Lemonade currently has no mechanism to identify or capitalize on this window. The Renewal Intelligence Engine solves this by collecting one simple data point - when does your car insurance renew? - and building an activation system around it.

🎯

Objective

Increase the renters-to-car bundle conversion rate by capturing car insurance renewal dates and activating personalized cross-sell offers 30 days before renewal - when switching intent naturally peaks.

👥

Target Audience

Existing Lemonade renters-only customers who already have car insurance with a competitor, are in a state where Lemonade offers car insurance (~60% of the base), and whose renewal window is approaching within 30 days.

⚡

Why This Wins

Cross-sold customers cost effectively $0 to acquire, generate 3× the premium of single-policy holders, and retain at 91–95% vs. 67–85%. This feature doesn't create new demand - it captures existing demand at the moment it peaks.

🔑

Value Exchange

The customer gives data now but gets value later — a thin exchange for a $15/month policyholder. V1 reduces friction (one-tap, month-level) and frames it as a service ("see what bundling could look like for you"), but also A/B tests a small immediate incentive (e.g., $5 Giveback bonus or one free month) against promise-only framing to measure what the trust gap actually costs to close.

The missing question that changes everything

Lemonade already identifies car owners during the renters flow - that's the hard part. But when a customer says "Yes, I have a car" and doesn't immediately bundle, the system simply moves on. No one asks "When does your current car insurance renew?" - the single data point that would let Lemonade follow up at exactly the right moment.

How It Works
Three Steps, One Goal: Right Offer, Right Moment
The engine does three things — ask when their car insurance renews, wait for the right moment, then surface a pre-filled quote when switching intent is highest. Phase 1 keeps it simple: rules-based logic, no ML, no over-engineering.
Phase 1
Core Engine
Ship the foundation — collect, trigger, convert
📅

1. Collect the Date

Maya asks "When does your car insurance renew?" during renters onboarding. If the customer doesn't know, we infer from purchase timing. A dashboard widget provides a passive fallback.

Target: 35–45% capture rate
⏰

2. Trigger at 30 Days Out

When renewal is 30 days away, the engine activates — the dashboard widget transforms into a countdown and one personalized push notification fires. Simple rules: eligible state? Not previously dismissed twice? Go.

Target: 20–30% engagement
🚗

3. Deliver an Express Quote

Both surfaces deep-link to a pre-filled car quote using existing renters data (name, address, DOB). Reduces the flow from ~10 steps to ~3 steps. Estimated 60–90 seconds vs. 5–10 minutes from scratch.

Target: 25–30% conversion
Built-In Guardrails
Respectful by Design
The engine is designed to never nag. Two simple rules keep it in check.
🔄

Dismiss-Twice Suppression

Customer ignores the widget and push this cycle? We wait for the next renewal window. Two consecutive dismissals trigger suppression until they show fresh intent (e.g., browsing the car tab). No nagging, ever.

📱

Saved Progress on Drop-Off

Customer taps "See My Quote" but doesn't finish? Their quote progress is saved in the dashboard widget so they can pick up where they left off — no pressure, no re-start.

Risk Mitigation
Anticipated Risks & Mitigations
High Risk
Thin Value Exchange at Point of Ask

At the moment Maya asks for the renewal date, the customer gets a promise — not a reward. For a $15/month policyholder, "tell me when your insurance renews and I'll message you in 6 months" is a lot of trust to extend. Mitigation: V1 runs a three-arm A/B test: promise-only, instant savings estimate, and a small immediate incentive ($5 Giveback bonus or one free month of renters). This lets us measure exactly how much the trust gap costs to close — and whether the promise-only framing is sufficient or needs reinforcement. If promise-only underperforms by >15%, the incentive arm becomes the default for V1 launch.

High Risk
Low Capture Rate

Customers may not share or know their renewal date. Mitigation: Month-level granularity (not exact date) reduces friction. Uncertain customers are inferred from purchase timing (see Flow 1). The Dashboard Calendar Widget provides a persistent secondary path.

Medium Risk
Push Notification Fatigue

Customers may perceive the notification as spam. Mitigation: V1 sends exactly one notification per cycle. "As promised" framing connects to the opt-in moment. Dismiss-twice suppression prevents re-targeting unhappy users.

Medium Risk
Price Competitiveness

Lemonade's car rates may not be competitive for every customer — and since we don't know their current premium, we can't pre-screen. Mitigation: Lead with the guaranteed 10% bundle discount, not savings claims. If the customer sees the quote and declines, Maya respects the decision gracefully and re-queues them for next cycle. The bundle discount remains a structural advantage.

Medium Risk
State Availability Gap

Car insurance only available in ~10 states (~60% of base). Mitigation: Only collect and activate for eligible customers. For ineligible states, store the data - it becomes instantly valuable when Lemonade expands to that state.

Medium Risk
Regulatory Compliance

Insurance marketing is regulated by state - specific pricing claims in push notifications may face scrutiny in certain jurisdictions. Mitigation: Legal review required on all push copy before launch. Messaging leads with the bundle discount (a verifiable, guaranteed offer) rather than savings estimates. State-specific copy variants where required.
