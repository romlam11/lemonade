Growth PM Assignment — Lemonade Insurance

Renewal Interceptor:
The right offer, at the right time

Lemonade's renters customers spend $3B/year on car insurance with competitors. The problem isn't the offer — it's the timing. This feature captures the renewal date of a customer's existing car policy and triggers a personalized cross-sell at the exact moment they're ready to switch.

Problem Statement
Lemonade has the customers. It just doesn't know when to ask.
With ~3M customers and only ~5% holding multiple policies, the cross-sell gap isn't about awareness — it's about timing. Today, Lemonade surfaces car insurance prompts randomly: a passive "+" icon, occasional emails, a generic Maya suggestion. But car insurance isn't an impulse buy. People switch at one predictable moment: renewal time.
The Core Insight
A perfectly crafted offer sent 3 months after renewal will be ignored.
The same offer sent 30 days before renewal converts at 5–10×.
Car insurance operates on rigid 6 or 12-month renewal cycles. Customers evaluate alternatives during a narrow window — 30 to 60 days before their policy expires. Outside that window, switching inertia is nearly insurmountable. Lemonade has no mechanism to identify or capitalize on this moment.

Product Strategy
One data point, five surfaces, one conversion engine
The Renewal Interceptor revolves around capturing a single data point — the customer's external car insurance renewal date — and then deploying it intelligently across every surface the customer touches.

How it works, in three steps

Step 1 — Collect: Ask renters customers when their car insurance renews. Do it naturally — during onboarding, during a claim interaction, or via a Maya prompt. No pressure, just a helpful question.

Step 2 — Store & Score: Feed the renewal date into Lemonade's Customer Cortex. Combine it with known data (address, age, car ownership signals) to build a conversion probability score.

Step 3 — Intercept: 30 days before renewal, trigger a multi-channel campaign: push notification, dashboard widget, in-app Maya message, and email — all personalized with the customer's current carrier, estimated savings, and a pre-filled express quote.

Who this is for

Primary: Existing renters-only customers in car-eligible states (~60% of the base since Colorado launch). They already trust Lemonade but haven't been given a compelling reason to switch — at the right time.

Guardrail Metrics — What We Protect

NPS Impact ≥ 0: The feature must not decrease customer satisfaction. If cross-sell prompts feel pushy, we pull back frequency.

Loss Ratio ≤ 75%: Renewal-triggered policies shouldn't attract worse risk profiles. We monitor for adverse selection.

Renters Churn ≤ Baseline: If customers feel over-solicited and cancel their renters policy, the feature has failed its core purpose.

Implementation Roadmap
Three phases — fast start, compounding impact
Phase 1 — Collect

Add the renewal question

Integrate the "When does your car insurance renew?" question into the renters purchase flow, Maya conversations, and claims resolution screens. Store in Customer Cortex. No new infrastructure needed — just CRM data capture.

4–6 weeks · Low engineering lift
Phase 2 — Trigger

Build the timing engine

Create the 30-day countdown trigger system. Push notifications, dashboard widget, and email campaign — all personalized with carrier name and estimated savings. Integrate with pricing engine for pre-calculated quotes.

8–10 weeks · Medium lift
Phase 3 — Optimize

ML-driven timing & scoring

Feed conversion data back into the model. Learn which customers convert at 30 days vs. 45 vs. 15. A/B test messaging, savings framing, and trigger frequency. Build propensity scoring to prioritize high-intent customers.

Ongoing · Compounds over time

Why this feature, and why now

Lemonade is already investing in an enhanced cross-sell platform for 2026. The Renewal Interceptor should be its centerpiece because it solves the fundamental timing problem — not the awareness problem, not the discount problem, not the product problem. Lemonade has great products and loyal customers. It just needs to ask at the right moment.

This feature is low-risk (Phase 1 is a simple data capture question), high-leverage (5–10× conversion vs. random timing), and defensible (every renewal date captured builds a compounding data moat that competitors can't replicate).
