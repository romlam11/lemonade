Current State, Problem & Opportunity

Lemonade's land-and-expand model acquires customers through cheap renters policies (~$5/mo) with the goal of growing them into multi-product households. The cross-sell engine is working — but leaving massive value on the table.

The Problem

Despite 91% of consumers saying they'd consider bundling, 8 out of 10 never buy a second product. Lemonade's cross-sell process is disjointed: no renewal timing intelligence, no competitive price comparison, and the full cold quote flow restarts from scratch — even for existing customers who've already shared their personal data.

80% never buy a second product

The Opportunity

Over half of new car policies already come from existing customers at zero CAC. Car cross-sales grew 100%+ YoY in Q1 2025. Bundled customers retain at 91-95% vs. 82% for single-policy — and drive ~20% of in-force premium with just ~5% of accounts. Every point of cross-sell improvement compounds across retention, LTV, and premium growth.

Bundled customers = 7.0yr avg tenure

Key Gaps Holding Back Conversion

1

No Renewal Timing Intelligence

Lemonade doesn't know when a customer's external car insurance renews — the optimal switching moment. Intent peaks 30-60 days before renewal; a well-timed offer converts at 5-10x the rate of random outreach.

High Impact
2

Full Cold Quote Flow for Existing Customers

Existing renters customers are forced through the same 10+ step car insurance flow as strangers — re-entering name, address, DOB, and contact info that Lemonade already has. Every unnecessary field causes 10-20% more drop-offs.

High Impact
3

No Competitive Price Comparison

Lemonade never asks "what do you currently pay?" — so it can't show a side-by-side savings view. Customers can't easily see the value of switching without doing the math themselves.

High Impact
4

Generic Cross-Sell Surfaces

Current cross-sell relies on static in-app icons and generic push notifications. No lifecycle-aware triggers, no personalized messaging, and no contextual entry points based on customer behavior or events.

Medium Impact

Today's Cross-Sell Experience — Real Screenshots

I went through Lemonade's actual renters onboarding to document the current cross-sell flow. These screenshots show both what's working and where the gaps live.

1. Product Selection
Clean multi-product picker with checkbox cards. The foundation exists — but no "Bundle" tags appear until you select the first product. Cold start problem.
2. Bundle Tags Appear
After selecting Renters, Car and Pet show pink "Bundle" tags — a good nudge. But there's no savings preview, no dollar amount, and no urgency to add a second product.
3. Bundle Unlocked
"Bundle discount unlocked!" with a gem icon — nice micro-celebration. But the flow now requires completing TWO full quote journeys sequentially. This is where drop-off spikes.
4. Bundle Offer Pitch
Maya pitches bundling benefits — but with generic bullets, not personalized savings. No dollar amount. No comparison to current provider. "Just Renters" is an easy escape hatch.
5. Late-Funnel Car Ask
"Do you have a car?" appears in final details — but only as a Yes/No. No follow-up on current carrier, renewal date, or what they pay. Critical data left uncollected.
6. Post-Quote Upsell
"Supercharge your policy" screen offers Renters+Car bundle as a toggle — but it's buried after checkout with no savings preview. The toggle is off by default.
Key Takeaway

The building blocks exist — product selection, bundle tags, Maya conversations — but the experience is sequential, generic, and missing critical data capture. There's no renewal timing, no competitive price comparison, no personalized savings preview, and the full car quote flow restarts from scratch even for existing customers.

Three Competitive Insights That Shaped The Solution

Insight 1 — Timing Wins

Jerry's PriceProtect™ built a $450M business on renewal timing

Jerry automatically re-shops insurance every 6 months before renewal, creating the perfect switching moment. Lemonade has zero renewal intelligence today — this is the single biggest gap vs. the market.

5-10× higher conversion at renewal window
Insight 2 — Friction Kills

Branch delivers a bundled price in 30 seconds — no re-entry

Branch auto-pulls public records to pre-fill data and returns an instant bundled price. Optimized form design produces a 42% conversion lift in auto insurance flows. Lemonade forces existing customers through the same cold flow as strangers.

42% conversion lift from optimized forms
Insight 3 — UX Over Discount

Lemonade's 10% discount trails Allstate's 25% — but UX can compensate

Analytics-driven personalized recommendations convert at 70% vs. 30-40% for generic offers (McKinsey). Lemonade's AI-native architecture and single-app ecosystem give it a structural UX advantage no traditional carrier can replicate — if it builds the right cross-sell experience.

70% conversion with AI-personalized offers

Section 4

Rollout Plan

A phased approach that delivers value fast, validates hypotheses early, and scales based on data. Each phase builds on the previous one's learnings and infrastructure.
Phase 1 — Foundation
Weeks 1–4
Renewal Data Collection

Add the "Do you have car insurance?" and "When does it renew?" questions to the Maya onboarding flow. Quick-reply format. Begin building the renewal timing database.

Express Quote Backend

Build the data-prefill pipeline: pull name, address, DOB, and contact info from renters policy into car quote flow. Reduce form fields from 10+ to 3-4.

A/B Test Setup

Instrument the funnel: onboarding data capture rate, notification opt-in rate, express quote start rate. Set up cohort tracking for Phase 2 comparison.

Phase 2 — Smart Triggers
Weeks 5–8
Renewal Notification Engine

Build the 30-day pre-renewal push notification system. Personalized messaging with carrier name, estimated savings, and deep link to express quote. A/B test notification copy and timing (30 vs. 45 vs. 60 days).

Switch & Save Screen

Build the side-by-side price comparison view. Capture current premium in the express flow, display the savings breakdown with "renters is practically free" framing.

Limited Launch

Roll out to 10% of existing renters customers in top 3 car-available states (Texas, Illinois, Ohio). Monitor conversion rates, notification engagement, and drop-off points.

Phase 3 — Full Launch & Retention
Weeks 9–12
Bundle Dashboard & Gamification

Launch the unified bundle dashboard with cumulative savings tracker, bundle status badges, and gem collection mechanic. Build the retention loop that drives 91%+ retention.

Scale to 100%

Roll out to all renters customers in car-available states. Launch referral sharing ("Share & Give $10 Off") from the celebration screen.

Lifecycle Expansion

Add secondary triggers: address change, turning 25, marriage, new car purchase. Backfill renewal data for existing customers via in-app survey or Maya check-in.

Phase 4 — Optimization & Next Products
Month 4+
ML-Powered Targeting

Train a propensity model on conversion data from Phases 2-3. Predict which customers are most likely to convert and optimize notification timing, messaging, and discount presentation.

Cross-Sell to Pet & Life

Extend the Smart Bundle Engine to Pet and Life insurance. The gem collection mechanic and express quote infrastructure already support it — just add product-specific questions.

What "Success" Looks Like

Within 6 months of full launch, the multi-policy ratio moves from ~5% to 8-10%. Express quote completion is 2× the cold flow. Bundled customers retain at 91%+ vs. 82% baseline. The Smart Bundle Engine becomes the #1 source of car policy acquisition, surpassing paid channels entirely.

Guardrail Metrics

Monitor for degradation in renters onboarding completion (the data capture step shouldn't add friction), notification opt-out rates (stay below 5%), and customer satisfaction scores. If NPS drops for any cohort exposed to the Smart Trigger, pause and iterate on messaging before scaling.
